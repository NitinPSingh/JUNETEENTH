June 12, 1862 — Written in Haste
To my dearest child, June,

If you are reading this, the Mayor kept his promise — and you have grown brave enough to seek your own answers. I always knew you would.

Years ago, when the danger became too great, I had to make the hardest choice of my life. I kept you safe by placing you with a kind family who promised to raise you away from the shadows of my past.

I was a bookkeeper for Whitmore & Sons. I uncovered they were moving enslaved people hidden beneath tobacco barrels. When they found out I knew, they sent men after me. But as Abraham Lincoln has now announced freedom for the enslaved, I think soon all these hurdles will be over.

Right now, I am actively escaping this place with my trusted friend Dinesh. Your mother and sister are alive. I am going to find them. Follow the trail I have left you — we will be together again.

With all the love a father carries,
— Your Father

June 17, 1865 — Written in Haste
My dearest Clara,

The war has torn everything open. Lincoln's proclamation reached us, but the roads are not safe — the Whitmore men turned violent the moment they lost their hold on us.

I am coming to you and Lily. I know the journey is dangerous, but I cannot wait any longer. June is protected — I placed her with a good family before I had to run. She will find her way to us.

Stay inside. Keep Lily close. I will be at the river landing before the week is out. Watch for me at the water's edge. I have left a hidden message in the pattern below — read it when you need strength.

With every breath of love,
— Your Husband

JUNE 21, 1865

THE FAMILY REUNITED

At the river's edge, as morning mist clears from the water, three familiar shapes appear — her mother, little Lily, and the father who spent three years leaving breadcrumbs home. June runs. There are no words, only arms around each other, tears, and sunlight. Freedom has arrived, and it brought the whole family with it.
